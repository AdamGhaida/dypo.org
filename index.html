Projects

DYPO's tangible impact.

Madrasatie

The chair of DYPO, Adam Abu Ghaida, developed Madrasatie alongside Massarat, an application catered to K through 12 learning. This project impacted 30+ state schools and 1000+ Druze students.

Druze Pioneer Program

With the help of board member Nidal Abou Zaki, DYPO delivered a summer course about the fundementals of Python, the most famous coding language in the world.

Global Learning

DYPO helped pioneer a LLM for teachers inside of Lebanon that helped solve crucial pedagogical problems like instructional downtime and lesson-prep. Using Anthropic's Claude2, the LLM helped small-town teachers prepare their students for global challenges, in a more full way.

DruzeTalks

Every month, The DYPO hosts seminars to help spread knowledge through online conferences. These conferences develop knowledge and skills inside the druze community.

Frequently Asked Questions

Being the smallest ethno-religious group in the world, we get lots of questions. Here are some of the most common ones.

1. Who are the Druze?

The Druze are an ethnoreligious group who follow the Tawheed Faith and the belief in the one-ness of a supreme being. Number around 800,000, they commonly reside in the levant however have sizable populations in North America and the Gulf.

2. What is the mission of The DYPO?

At the DYPO, we strive to give the historically under-represented Druze Populations with a platform to strive. We are a charitable non-profit organization headed by the Abu Ghaida family from Hasbaya, Lebanon dedicated to taking the Druze to new heights.

3. Do we accept volunteers?

Absolutely. By volunteering, you'll be able to contribute to DYPO's success. Head here to contact us.
